Minnesota's veteran suicides continue taking a steady toll

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune November 11, 2019

Recent state deaths data shows a downturn in veteran suicide rates in Minnesota, though 100 or more former service members still die by suicide annually throughout the state.

Preliminary death data from the Minnesota Department of Health shows 2018's suicide rate among those with prior military service – about 35 per 100,000 – still doubled rates in the civilian population, despite a decline from the previous year.

In all, about 1,500 Minnesota veterans have died by suicide at accelerating rates since 2005, underscoring a longstanding crisis that’s taken a heavy toll on vulnerable veteran populations while playing a role in worsening suicide numbers overall.

Minnesota's rising suicide rates
Minnesota's veteran suicide rate per 100,000 has been steadily climbing for decades, far surpassing the state's total and civilian rates.

Minnesota's general and veteran suicide rates have risen in tandem with national trends as public health officials struggle to find solutions.

And Minnesota's gradually dwindling veteran populations age, experts have warned rates could further rise without intervention.

Veteran suicide deaths in Minnesota tend to be among those who are male and older than 54, according to the U.S. Department of Veteran Affairs.

Two-thirds of Minnesota's veteran suicide deaths involved a firearm, a method more common among former service members than other groups.

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs estimates about 17 military veterans die of suicide each day nationally.

The Veterans Crisis Line can be reached toll free by calling 1-800-273-8255 and pressing 1. It’s open to all veterans, not just those who are patients at VA medical centers.

The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is 1-800-273-8255.

The Crisis Text Line is 741-741.
