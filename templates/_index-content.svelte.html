Minnesota's hidden veteran deathtoll continues its rise

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune November 11, 2019

As Minnesota's overall suicide rate increases overall, the rate among the state's military veterans has risen at faster rates, underscoring the greater risk those who have returned from service face.

Suicides in Minnesota increased about five percent in the total population from 2016 to 2017, according to the Minnesota Department of Health. Over that time period, VA numbers show veteran suicide rates jumped TK to TK, nearly three times the overall population.

Minnesota's veteran suicide rate per 100,000 has been steadily climbing for decades, well surpassing the state's total suicide rate.
Data source: Minnesota Department of Health, U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs

Minnesota's overall suicide rate has risen in tandem with national rates, nearly matching them. Minnesota's veteran suicide rate has also been in-step with national trends.

Veterans in Minnesota dying by suicide tend to be older than 54, according to the VA. About 55 percent of Minnesota's veteran suicide deaths involved a firearm, compared to 45 percent in the state's overall population.
