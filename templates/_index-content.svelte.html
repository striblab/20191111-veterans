Minnesota's veteran suicides continue taking a steady toll

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune November 11, 2019

As Minnesota’s aging veteran population gradually dwindles, 100 or more former service members still die by suicide each year – doubling rates in the civilian and overall populations.

About 1,500 veterans have died by suicide in Minnesota since 2005, at rates that climbed 10 points across the last decade, underscoring a longstanding crisis that’s taken a steady toll on vulnerable veteran populations while playing a role in worsening suicide numbers overall.

And while 2018's veteran suicide rate dropped about three points from the prior year, it remained far higher than rates in other populations.

Minnesota's rising suicide rates
Minnesota's veteran suicide rate per 100,000 has been steadily climbing for decades, far surpassing the state's total and civilian rates.

Minnesota's general and veteran suicide rates have risen in tandem with national trends as public health officials struggle to find solutions.

And as veteran populations age, particularly those from post-9/11 wars, experts said rates could further rise without intervention.

Veterans in Minnesota dying by suicide tend to be male and older than 54, according to the U.S. Department of Veteran Affairs.

More than half of Minnesota's veteran suicide deaths involved a firearm, a method more common among former service members than the general population.
